The biggest source of carbon dioxide emissions in your sushi-rrito came from _____. In general, fish ingredients tend to have a lower carbon footprint compared to meat ingredients such as beef. However, vegetarian ingredients have the lowest carbon footprint of them all and consuming vegetarian ingredients tends to have the least negative impact on the environment. Additionally, fish and fish byproduct ingredients can be high in chemicals such as mercury, which are detrimental to your health and should not be consumed in excess. Consider swapping out fish or meat ingredients for vegetarian ingredients the next time you eat out to minimize negative health and environmental impacts!
